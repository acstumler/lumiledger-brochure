The thesis

Small-business financial problems persist not because owners ignore their finances, but because financial data is not structured to support professional review or informed decision-making.

LumiLedger addresses this structural gap by preparing and interpreting financial data before accounting work begins.

This is the layer that has been missing.

The Problem in Small-Business Financials

A structural data problem, not a lack of effort or tools.

Where the breakdown starts

Most small businesses do not struggle because they ignore their finances. They struggle because the financial data they generate is not structured in a way that supports professional review or informed decision-making.

For the majority of small businesses, records consist primarily of bank and credit card transactions spread across multiple accounts, platforms, and statements. While this activity reflects real economic behavior, it is rarely organized into a clean, consistent format before it reaches an accountant or advisor.

Fragmented across multiple sources
Inconsistently categorized
Poorly structured at the General Ledger level
Difficult to trace back to original activity

Before any advisory, tax, or planning work can begin, a significant amount of time is spent reconstructing what already happened. This recurring cleanup is not an edge case; it is the norm.

Who this problem affects

Small businesses account for 99.9% of all U.S. businesses, with the vast majority employing fewer than 25 people. Firms of this size typically operate without internal accounting teams and rely on external CPAs or bookkeepers for financial review, compliance, and guidance.

Within this segment, simplicity is not a preference; it is a necessity.

Most businesses with 1–25 employees manage their finances using cash-basis accounting, recording income and expenses when money is received or paid. Industry research consistently indicates that approximately two-thirds of U.S. small businesses use the cash method, particularly among smaller, owner-managed firms.

Cash-basis accounting aligns closely with how owners experience their businesses day-to-day. However, without proper preparation, it often produces financial records that are informal, inconsistent, difficult to standardize, and time-consuming to review professionally.

These businesses are simple in theory, but messy in practice.

Why data quality matters

The consequences of disorganized financial data extend beyond bookkeeping inconvenience.

Research from the JPMorgan Chase Institute shows that roughly half of small businesses operate with fewer than 15 days of cash buffer, making timely and accurate financial insight critical. Federal Reserve surveys further highlight rising operating costs, uneven cash flows, and increasing reliance on external advisors across this segment.

When financial data is unclear or unreliable:

Business owners struggle to understand performance
Advisors spend time fixing inputs instead of advising
Lending, planning, and strategy conversations are delayed or weakened

The cost is not simply inefficiency; it is missed clarity at moments when clarity matters most.

Why this problem persists

This challenge is not caused by a lack of accounting software, bank feeds, or bookkeeping services. Small businesses already use all of these tools.

What is missing is a dedicated financial data preparation layer, one designed specifically to take raw transaction activity and organize it into a clean, review-ready structure before professional accounting work begins.

LumiLedger addresses the structural gap between raw small-business transaction data and the clean financial inputs required for effective professional review.

How LumiLedger Fills the Gap

Small-business financial data does not fail because it is wrong; it fails because it is unprepared.

Why preparation matters

Before accounting judgment can be applied, raw transaction activity must be organized, structured, and made understandable. Without this step, accounting professionals are forced to spend time reconstructing the past instead of advising on the future.

Solving this problem does not require another accounting system. It requires a disciplined way to prepare financial data before professional work begins.

That is the role LumiLedger is designed to play.

A preparation and interpretation layer, not an accounting system

LumiLedger is not a traditional accounting system or a bookkeeping service. It is a financial preparation, reporting, and interpretation layer that sits between raw transaction data and professional accounting judgment.

LumiLedger can generate accurate financial statements for understanding and decision-making, but it does not replace a firm's system of record, enforce accounting elections, or assert compliance authority. Final judgment and sign-off always remain with the accounting professional.

This distinction is intentional.

How LumiLedger works

1. Account connection

Business owners connect the financial accounts used in the business, including bank accounts, credit cards, and loans. These accounts establish the known sources of financial activity.

LumiLedger uses Plaid Secure, API-based data sharing for all financial connections, ensuring security, stability, and efficiency in how transaction data is accessed and maintained.

2. Transaction ingestion and journal logic

Each transaction is evaluated using accounting logic. One side of the journal entry is anchored to a known source account (such as a bank or credit card liability). The offsetting side is automatically classified using machine-learned and AI-assisted logic. All classifications are transparent, reviewable, and adjustable.

3. Financial statement generation

From these journal entries, LumiLedger generates structured financial statements, including Income Statements, Balance Sheets, and General Ledger views, for analysis, review, and discussion.

4. KPI calculation and financial storytelling

LumiLedger calculates key performance indicators in the background and generates a plain-English financial story that explains what is happening in the business.

Day-to-day and week-to-week activity
Month-over-month and quarter-over-quarter trends
Year-over-year changes
Notable shifts in cash flow, spending, or performance

The result is clarity without complexity.

Simplicity without oversimplification

Most financial software assumes accounting fluency and presents dense tables, charts, and metrics with little explanation. LumiLedger takes the opposite approach.

Accounting structure is preserved in the background. On the surface, only what matters now is explained clearly and in plain language.

This allows business owners to stay informed without being inundated, while preserving the rigor professionals expect.

Data completeness and transparency

LumiLedger does not assume financial data is complete. It is source-driven.

Every transaction is tied to a connected account, making coverage explicit. If an account is not connected or activity is missing, that gap remains visible rather than silently ignored. Financial narratives are written in the context of the data that exists.

LumiLedger does not promise perfect inputs; it prevents undetected incompleteness.

Personal accounts used for business

LumiLedger is designed for real-world small businesses and assumes that personal accounts are often used for business activity.

Personal bank or credit card accounts can be connected intentionally. Transactions are classified individually, allowing business and personal activity to be separated at the transaction level. Commingling is surfaced transparently rather than hidden.

This preserves economic accuracy while allowing accounting professionals to advise appropriately.

When LumiLedger is sufficient and when it hands off

For many small, cash-basis businesses, LumiLedger can serve as the primary financial tool for ongoing visibility, internal decision-making, advisor conversations, and lender preparation.

When formal compliance, filing, or a system of record is required, LumiLedger hands off clean, well-structured data to an accounting platform such as QuickBooks, where professional judgment is finalized.

This flexibility allows LumiLedger to meet businesses where they are and grow with them.

What LumiLedger does and does not do

LumiLedger does

  • Apply consistent journal-entry logic
  • Preserve traceability between transactions and sources
  • Generate structured financial statements for analysis
  • Translate performance into plain-English insight

LumiLedger does not

  • Replace bookkeeping or accounting services
  • Make tax or accounting judgments
  • Serve as the system of record
  • Eliminate the need for professional review

Final authority always remains with the accounting professional.

How LumiLedger Is Built to Be Used and to Grow

LumiLedger is being developed with a deliberate, phased approach that reflects how small businesses operate and how accounting professionals adopt new tools.

Why the phased approach matters

The goal is not rapid expansion at the expense of trust, but steady adoption grounded in real-world use.

Phase 1: Working alongside small accounting firms

LumiLedger is initially designed to work with small accounting firms that serve owner-managed businesses in the 1–25 employee range.

Regularly receive incomplete or disorganized financial data
Spend significant time on cleanup before higher-value work begins
Balance bookkeeping, review, and advisory services across a wide range of client sophistication

In this phase, LumiLedger is introduced to clients through trusted advisors or used collaboratively as a preparation tool. Clients benefit from clearer financial visibility, while firms receive cleaner, more consistent inputs.

This phase prioritizes accuracy and transparency, real advisor feedback, alignment with existing workflows, and validation from daily use, not theoretical scale.

Phase 2: Firm partnerships and selective white-label use

As LumiLedger matures, it supports deeper firm-level use for practices that want standardization across clients.

Firm-specific views or dashboards
Custom Chart of Accounts mappings
Co-branded or white-label environments
Tailored workflows aligned with firm processes

White labeling is optional and selective, not required. LumiLedger is designed to remain flexible, allowing firms to adopt it in the way that best fits their practice.

The intent is to support firms that see LumiLedger as infrastructure, not to force a single operating model.

Phase 3: Direct-to-consumer expansion

Direct-to-consumer use becomes appropriate once the product is fully validated through professional use and supported by sufficient marketing and operational resources.

Reach business owners earlier
Improve baseline financial literacy
Reduce confusion before advisor engagement
Scale access to clear financial understanding

Importantly, this phase builds on rather than bypasses professional involvement. LumiLedger remains compatible with accountants and advisors, regardless of how a client first encounters the product.

A product that grows with the business

Some businesses will use LumiLedger as their primary financial tool. Others will use it as a preparation layer feeding into a formal accounting system.

LumiLedger is designed to support both paths.

By focusing first on clarity, structure, and transparency, LumiLedger meets businesses where they are today while remaining adaptable as their needs evolve.
