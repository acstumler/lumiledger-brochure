For Accounting Professionals

How LumiLedger fits into real firm workflows and preserves professional judgment

The problem LumiLedger solves for you

You regularly receive incomplete, disorganized, or poorly structured financial data from small-business clients.

Before advisory or compliance work can begin, you spend time reconstructing transactions, chasing down missing information, and organizing activity that should have been prepared before reaching you.

LumiLedger addresses this preparation gap.

What LumiLedger provides

LumiLedger prepares small-business financial data into clean, review-ready inputs. Transactions are classified, journal entries are generated, and financial statements are structured before reaching your desk.

Your role shifts from data cleanup to professional review, adjustment, and advisory.

This is not automation for automation's sake. It is preparation that respects your time and judgment.

How LumiLedger fits into your workflow

Client onboarding

You introduce LumiLedger to clients who need better financial visibility or whose records require regular cleanup. Setup takes minutes.

Ongoing preparation

LumiLedger imports transactions, classifies activity, and generates financial statements automatically. Clients gain visibility. You receive cleaner inputs.

Review and adjustment

You review prepared data, adjust classifications as needed, and apply professional judgment where required. Time previously spent on cleanup is now available for higher-value work.

Handoff to system of record

When formal compliance or filing is required, you export clean data from LumiLedger into QuickBooks or another accounting platform where final judgment is applied.

Professional judgment is preserved

LumiLedger does not make accounting judgments, tax elections, or compliance decisions.

It prepares data. You apply judgment.

This boundary is intentional and reflects a commitment to supporting your authority, not replacing it.

Transparency and reviewability

Every transaction classification in LumiLedger is transparent, reviewable, and adjustable. You can see why a classification was applied, what confidence level was assigned, and where uncertainty exists.

You are never forced to trust a black box. The system is designed for professional review.

Handling client commingling

LumiLedger is built for the reality that many small-business clients use personal accounts for business activity.

Personal accounts can be connected intentionally, and transactions are classified at the transaction level. This allows you to see complete activity and advise appropriately without requiring clients to change their behavior first.

When LumiLedger is sufficient

For many cash-basis, owner-managed businesses, LumiLedger provides sufficient structure for ongoing visibility, internal decision-making, and advisor conversations.

Clients who do not need formal compliance systems can use LumiLedger as their primary financial tool.

You remain involved as needed for advisory, tax planning, and periodic review.

When handoff is required

When formal compliance, filing, or a system of record is required, LumiLedger hands off clean, structured data to your accounting platform.

This flexibility allows you to use LumiLedger as a preparation layer for some clients and as a primary tool for others, depending on their needs.

Firm-level use and customization

As your use of LumiLedger matures, firm-level customization becomes available.

Firm-specific Chart of Accounts mappings
Standardized workflows across clients
Firm dashboards for portfolio visibility
Co-branded or white-label environments
Tailored classification rules aligned with your practice

These features are optional. LumiLedger is designed to fit your practice, not force a single operating model.

Client communication and education

LumiLedger generates plain-English financial narratives that help clients understand their performance without requiring accounting fluency.

This improves the quality of advisory conversations by ensuring clients arrive prepared with baseline clarity.

You spend less time explaining basics and more time advising on what matters.

Pricing and client billing

LumiLedger can be introduced to clients as part of your service offering or billed separately depending on your practice model.

Pricing is structured to support both approaches.

Your relationship with the client remains primary. LumiLedger is infrastructure, not a competing service.

Data security and access control

All financial data is encrypted and secured. You control which clients you have access to and what level of visibility you maintain.

Clients retain ownership of their data. You are granted access as their advisor.

This structure preserves professional boundaries and data governance standards.

Support and onboarding

LumiLedger provides direct support for accounting professionals adopting the platform.

Onboarding includes workflow consultation, setup assistance, and ongoing access to product updates and best practices.

You are not expected to figure this out alone. Support is structured to help you integrate LumiLedger into your practice effectively.

Why early adoption matters

Accounting professionals who adopt LumiLedger early gain influence over product development, access to firm-level features, and positioning as leaders in financial data preparation.

Early feedback directly shapes how the product evolves.

LumiLedger is being built in partnership with practitioners, not in isolation from them.

Long-term value

LumiLedger is designed to reduce recurring data cleanup work, improve client clarity, and shift your time toward advisory services.

Over time, this shift strengthens client relationships, increases the value of your services, and allows you to serve more clients without proportional increases in administrative burden.

This is infrastructure that scales with your practice.
