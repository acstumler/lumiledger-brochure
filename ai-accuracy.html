AI accuracy and compliance

LumiLedger is built with the assumption that accuracy, explainability, and professional responsibility matter more than novelty.

AI is used selectively and deliberately — only where it improves clarity or efficiency without undermining trust. Where certainty, consistency, or accountability are required, LumiLedger relies on deterministic logic and human review.

This balance is intentional.

AI as assistance, not authority

LumiLedger does not treat AI as an autonomous decision-maker.

AI is used to assist with:

Pattern recognition
Classification suggestions
Narrative explanation and summarization

AI is not used to:

Make final accounting judgments
Enforce accounting policies
Assert compliance or filing readiness
Replace professional review

Final authority always remains with the accounting professional.

Using the right logic for the right task

LumiLedger uses different types of logic depending on the nature of the task:

Deterministic logic

Used where outcomes must be exact and explainable, such as journal-entry structure, arithmetic consistency, and statement assembly.

Predictive models

Used to assist with classification tasks where patterns are repetitive and well-defined. These outputs are treated as suggestions and remain reviewable.

Generative models

Used only for interpretation and communication — translating structured financial data into plain-English explanations.

This separation ensures that accuracy-critical workflows are never dependent on probabilistic outputs alone.

Accuracy without false confidence

LumiLedger does not claim perfect accuracy.

Instead, it is designed to:

  • Preserve uncertainty where it exists
  • Surface ambiguity rather than conceal it
  • Allow human review and correction
  • Improve over time through structured feedback

Outputs are intended to improve clarity and reduce noise — not to eliminate the need for professional oversight.

Reliability comes from transparency, not overconfidence.

Transparency and explainability

All AI-assisted outputs in LumiLedger are explainable and traceable back to underlying data.

This includes:

  • Visibility into source accounts
  • Traceability from transactions to journal entries
  • Clear separation between system logic and AI-assisted interpretation

LumiLedger avoids black-box behavior. If a result cannot be explained, it is not treated as authoritative.

Alignment with professional guidance

LumiLedger's approach to AI aligns with the guidance published by CPA.com and the AICPA on responsible AI use in accounting and advisory contexts.

In particular, LumiLedger reflects shared professional principles emphasized in CPA.com resources, including:

Using deterministic logic where accuracy is critical
Reserving generative AI for communication and explanation
Maintaining human-in-the-loop review
Prioritizing transparency, auditability, and control

These resources provide evaluation frameworks for accounting firms assessing AI-enabled tools. LumiLedger is designed to operate comfortably within those expectations.

Clear boundaries and responsibility

LumiLedger is intentionally bounded.

It does not:

  • Serve as a system of record
  • Make tax or accounting elections
  • Assert compliance readiness
  • Replace professional judgment

These boundaries reduce risk, preserve flexibility, and ensure LumiLedger remains compatible with firm workflows and professional standards.
