AI Accuracy & Compliance

How AI is used responsibly, transparently, and in alignment with professional standards

AI as implementation, not identity

LumiLedger uses AI to assist with transaction classification and narrative generation. This is an implementation detail, not the product itself.

The product is financial data preparation. AI is one of several tools used to achieve that goal.

This distinction is intentional.

Where AI is used

AI is applied in two primary areas within LumiLedger:

Transaction classification: AI assists in determining the appropriate account category for the offsetting side of each journal entry.

Narrative generation: AI generates plain-English explanations of financial trends, performance changes, and notable patterns.

Where AI is not used

AI does not make accounting judgments, tax elections, or compliance determinations.

AI does not infer missing data, estimate account balances, or fill gaps with assumptions.

AI does not replace professional review or override user corrections.

How transaction classification works

Each transaction imported into LumiLedger is evaluated for classification. The system uses a combination of machine-learned patterns and AI-assisted reasoning.

Pattern matching

The system first checks whether the transaction matches known patterns based on historical data, vendor memory, and user corrections.

AI reasoning

If no clear pattern exists, AI evaluates the transaction description, amount, and context to suggest a classification.

Confidence scoring

Each classification is assigned a confidence score. Low-confidence classifications are flagged for review.

User correction

Users and advisors can review, adjust, or override any classification. Corrections are stored and used to improve future accuracy.

Transparency over confidence

LumiLedger does not hide uncertainty. When classification confidence is low, that uncertainty is surfaced.

Transactions flagged for review are presented clearly. Users and advisors can prioritize attention where it is most needed.

This approach favors transparency over false confidence.

Learning from corrections

When a user or advisor corrects a classification, that correction is stored and applied to similar transactions in the future.

Over time, the system adapts to business-specific patterns, vendor naming conventions, and user preferences.

This learning process improves accuracy without reducing transparency.

Narrative generation

LumiLedger generates plain-English financial narratives to explain what is happening in the business.

These narratives are generated from structured financial data, not inferred from assumptions.

Narratives describe observed trends and changes. They do not make predictions or offer strategic advice.

Boundaries of narrative generation

Narratives explain what has happened, not what should happen.

They identify trends and changes, but do not interpret causation or recommend actions.

Strategic interpretation remains the domain of business owners and their advisors.

Accuracy and validation

Classification accuracy is measured and monitored continuously. Accuracy rates are reviewed regularly by accounting professionals during development.

Misclassifications are tracked, analyzed, and used to improve system logic.

LumiLedger does not claim perfect accuracy. It claims continuous improvement and transparent uncertainty.

Compliance boundaries

LumiLedger does not assert compliance authority. It does not make tax elections, enforce accounting standards, or certify financial statements.

Generated financial statements are for understanding and analysis, not for filing or formal compliance.

Final compliance authority remains with the accounting professional.

Professional review and sign-off

LumiLedger is designed to reduce the time required for professional review, not eliminate it.

Accounting professionals review prepared data, adjust as needed, and apply professional judgment before any formal use.

This workflow preserves professional authority and ensures that AI assists rather than replaces judgment.

Data privacy and AI training

Financial data processed by LumiLedger is not used to train third-party AI models.

Classification improvements are based on aggregated patterns, not individual transaction data.

User data remains private and is never sold or shared outside of the system.

Responsible AI principles

Transparency over black boxes
Uncertainty surfaced, not hidden
User corrections always respected
Professional judgment preserved
Continuous improvement, not false confidence
Data privacy protected

Ongoing evaluation

AI performance is evaluated continuously based on real-world use, advisor feedback, and measured accuracy.

System improvements are deployed regularly to refine classification logic, improve narrative quality, and reduce errors.

LumiLedger is built for continuous improvement, not one-time deployment.

When AI is not enough

LumiLedger recognizes that AI-assisted classification is not sufficient for all businesses or all transactions.

Complex accounting situations, unusual transactions, and industry-specific classifications require professional review.

LumiLedger prepares data for that review rather than attempting to bypass it.
